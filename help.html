Frequently Asked Questions

How do I generate a QR code?

Simply select the type of QR code you want (URL, WhatsApp, SMS, UPI, etc.), enter your data in the input fields, customize colors and size if desired, and click "Generate QR". Your QR code will appear instantly!

Can I scan QR codes from images?

Yes! You can either use your camera for live scanning, upload an image from your device, or drag and drop a QR code image directly into the scanner area. All methods work great!

Is my data safe and private?

Absolutely! All processing happens locally in your browser. We don't collect, store, or transmit any of your data to servers. Your information never leaves your device. Check our Privacy Policy for details.

What file formats can I download?

You can download your QR codes as PNG (for general use, web, social media) or SVG (for high-quality printing, scaling without losing quality). Both formats maintain excellent quality.

What is error correction level?

Error correction allows QR codes to be read even if they're partially damaged or obscured. Higher levels (H - 30%) allow more damage tolerance but create denser QR codes. Medium (M - 15%) is recommended for most uses.

Why isn't my WhatsApp QR working?

Make sure you enter the phone number with country code (e.g., +91 for India). Remove any spaces or special characters. The format should be like: +919876543210. Also ensure the number is registered on WhatsApp.

How does UPI QR code work?

UPI QR codes contain your UPI ID and optional amount. When scanned with any UPI app (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm), it auto-fills the payment details. The payer just needs to enter their PIN to complete payment.

Can I add a logo to my QR code?

Currently, we support color customization. For adding logos, use high error correction (H) level as it allows for more modifications while keeping the code scannable. You can overlay a small logo (up to 30% of QR area) using image editing software.

Why is my camera not working for scanning?

Make sure you've granted camera permission to the browser. On mobile, check Settings > Apps > Browser > Permissions. Also, the camera needs HTTPS to work (our site supports this). Try refreshing the page or using a different browser.

How long do QR codes last?

Static QR codes (like the ones we generate) never expire! They're just encoded data. However, if the content they link to (like a website) goes offline, the QR code becomes useless. The QR code itself remains valid forever.

Pros & Cons of QR Codes

Advantages

  • High Data Capacity

    Store up to 7,089 numeric or 4,296 alphanumeric characters

  • Fast Scanning

    Read in milliseconds from any angle

  • Error Correction

    Works even if up to 30% is damaged

  • Free to Use

    No cost to create or scan

  • Contactless

    No physical contact needed for data transfer

  • Versatile

    Works on screens, paper, products, anything

Disadvantages

  • Requires Device

    Need smartphone or scanner to read

  • Security Risks

    Can link to malicious websites or content

  • Not Human-Readable

    Can't tell what's inside without scanning

  • Lighting Dependent

    Poor lighting affects scanning

  • Replacement Risk

    Physical codes can be covered with fake ones

  • Internet Needed

    URL QRs need internet to work

Safety Tips

Verify Source

Only scan QR codes from trusted sources. Be suspicious of random QR codes in public places.

Preview URLs

Always check the URL preview before opening. Look for suspicious or misspelled domains.

Protect Sensitive Info

Never encode passwords, bank details, or personal info in publicly shared QR codes.

Keep Apps Updated

Use updated QR scanner apps and browsers for latest security patches.

Check for Tampering

Look for stickers placed over original QR codes. Scammers overlay fake codes.

Verify Payments

Double-check UPI ID and amount before confirming any payment via QR.

WiFi Caution

Be careful with public WiFi QR codes. They could connect you to malicious networks.

Email QR Codes

Don't trust QR codes in suspicious emails. They could be phishing attempts.
